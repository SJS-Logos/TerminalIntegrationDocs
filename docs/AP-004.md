# 1. Introduction

This AP expands the Domain principles of AP-001 (§6.3) and the Domain Unit structure of AP-002 (§5.1) into implementation guidance.

It defines how business rules, entities, policies, services, and outcomes are organized within the Domain Unit, and where the Domain's Capability abstractions and shared business datatypes (the Shared Kernel) reside.

---

# 2. Normative Language

The key words **MUST**, **MUST NOT**, **SHALL**, **SHOULD**, and **MAY** in this document are to be interpreted as described in BCP 14 (RFC 2119 [1] and RFC 8174 [2]) when, and only when, they appear in all capitals, as shown here.

---

# 3. Terminology

The logical terms **Entity**, **Value Object**, **Domain Service**, and **Business Decision** retain the meanings defined in AP-001 [3].

* **Outcome** — The business result produced by a Domain operation; the expression of a Business Decision (AP-001 §6.1).
* **Policy** — A named business rule or set of rules that governs whether and how a Business Decision is made.
* **Aggregate** — A cluster of Entities and Value Objects treated as a single consistency boundary for business operations.

---

# 4. Domain Contents

The Domain Unit SHALL contain only business meaning, expressed through the following elements:

* Business rules.
* Entities.
* Value Objects (see §7).
* Policies.
* Domain Services.
* Outcomes.

These elements divide into two categories, following AP-001 §6.3:

* **Business State** is modelled by Entities and Value Objects.
* **Business Behaviour** is modelled by business rules, Policies, and Domain Services.

The Domain SHALL keep Business State and Business Behaviour distinct so that behaviour can be verified independently of the transport and persistence mechanisms that carry state.

---

# 5. Decision Ownership

* Every business decision SHALL be made within exactly one Domain (AP-001 §6.1).
* A Domain operation SHALL express its result as an **Outcome** (§3): an explicit business value that names the decision reached, rather than a transport- or persistence-specific status.
* An Outcome SHALL be returned to the Application as a domain type; the Application translates it into a Contract Model response (AP-003), and Implementations translate it into transport-specific forms (AP-003, AP-007).
* An Outcome MUST NOT carry transport, persistence, or serialization concerns (§7.3).

---

# 6. Coordination

This AP resolves the coordination question deferred by AP-001 §6.2, which left open whether coordination between Domain operations belongs to the Application, to Domain Services, or elsewhere.

Coordination between Domain operations (across aggregates) SHALL be expressed by **Domain Services** within the Domain Unit, not by the Application.

* The Application invokes a single Domain entry point per use case.
* Domain Services own multi-aggregate coordination and the resulting business outcome.

## 6.1 Rationale

Placing coordination in Domain Services keeps every business decision — including decisions that span multiple aggregates — inside the Domain, preserving the Decision Ownership invariant (AP-001 §6.1). If the Application coordinated aggregates directly, the sequencing and the conditions under which each aggregate is invoked would become business behaviour living outside the Domain, which AP-001 §6.2 prohibits.

## 6.2 Boundaries

* A Domain Service SHALL coordinate only Domain elements (Entities, Value Objects, Policies, other Domain Services). It MUST NOT invoke the Application, a Host, or any Implementation.
* A Domain Service MAY depend on Capabilities (AP-005) to obtain what the Domain requires, but the decision it reaches remains within the Domain.
* Coordination that is purely mechanical wiring (dependency injection, dispatch) is not business coordination and belongs to the Application or Host, not to a Domain Service.

---

# 7. Value Objects and the Shared Kernel

A Value Object is an immutable, data-carrying business type identified by its values. Structurally, a Value Object differs little from a Message (AP-003): both are shapes of data. This AP therefore treats shared Value Objects as part of the **Shared Kernel** (AP-001 §6.4, AP-002 §5.1.2) so that a single definition serves both the Domain and the Application's Contract Models.

## 7.1 Shared Definition

* A shared Value Object SHALL be defined once as a data-carrying type and reused, rather than duplicated between the Domain and the incoming/outgoing contracts (AP-003).
* The incoming and response Messages of the Application (AP-003 §5) SHOULD reference these shared Value Object types rather than redeclare equivalent shapes.
* The intent is to **reduce duplication** between shared business datatypes and Contract Models.

## 7.2 Placement

* Shared Value Objects SHALL reside in the `SharedKernel/` folder of the Core Unit, a peer folder alongside `Domain/`, `Capabilities/`, and `Application/` (AP-002 §5.1.2).
* Because the Shared Kernel depends on nothing and the Domain, Capabilities, and Application MAY depend on it, dependency direction is preserved without introducing bidirectional coupling (AP-001 §6.4, AP-002 §7).
* Value Objects that are private to the Domain (not shared with contracts) MAY remain within the `Domain/` folder; only shared business datatypes belong in the Shared Kernel.

## 7.3 Purity

* A Value Object MUST remain a pure business type. It MUST NOT carry transport, persistence, or serialization concerns (framework attributes, wire formats, ORM mappings).
* Any serialization or transport binding SHALL be applied by the relevant Implementation (AP-003, AP-007), never within the Value Object definition.

## 7.4 Documentation

* Value Objects SHALL be documented at their definition, in the format required by the documentation tool (Swashbuckle/Doxygen), consistent with AP-003 §5.5.

---

# 8. Capabilities within the Domain

* Capability abstractions reside in the Core Unit's `Capabilities/` folder, a peer folder alongside `Domain/`, `SharedKernel/`, and `Application/` (AP-002 §5.1.3).
* Detailed capability expression is specified in AP-005.

---

# 9. Constraints

* The Domain MUST NOT depend on any Host or Implementation, and MUST NOT depend on the Application (AP-002 §7). The Domain MAY depend on the Shared Kernel and Capabilities (AP-001 §6.4, AP-002 §7).
* No transport, persistence, messaging, deployment, or framework constructs (AP-001 §6.3).

---

# 10. Compliance

A Domain implementation complies with this AP if:

* The Domain Unit contains only Business State and Business Behaviour, with the two kept distinct (§4).
* Every business decision is made within exactly one Domain and expressed as an Outcome (§5).
* Multi-aggregate coordination is expressed by Domain Services, with the Application invoking a single Domain entry point per use case (§6).
* Shared Value Objects are defined once in `SharedKernel/` and reused by Domain and Contract Models, rather than duplicated (§7).
* Value Objects and Outcomes remain pure business types, free of transport, persistence, and serialization concerns (§5, §7.3).
* Capability abstractions reside in the Core Unit's `Capabilities/` folder (§8).
* The Domain depends only on the Shared Kernel and Capabilities, and on nothing outside the Core (§9).
